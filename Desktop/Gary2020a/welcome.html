Welcome to Coats Shoeing

"Thank you for to all of our faithful customers over the years. We appreciate your business.
If you are in need of a horse shoer or foot corrections made, I am your guy."

Gary Coats

Horseshoer and roper Gary Coats and his wife Sharon, own and operate Coats Horseshoeing
in San Angelo, Texas. Gary has been shoeing since 1981 and is well known all over
West Texas for his shoeing expertise.

Born and raised in Nebraska, Gary traveled to Arizona late in 1980 to learn his trade at
Dale's Western Horseshoeing School in Phoenix where he completed the three-month course.

After receiving his diploma, they went back to Nebraska where Gary helped is brother,
Bill Coats Jr., shoe horses. They also ran Thoroughbreds and Quarterhorses on the track
where they stayed for eight years before heading to San Angelo to begin shoeing. Gary's
business exploded and he quickly became a top shoer. Gary specializes in corrective shoeing
and is the best at taking a horse with hoof problems and making him sound again.

Life has thrown a few punches at Gary over the years, but he has never been away from
shoeing more than a week at a time. Gary has never stopped in his 40+ years and he
won't stop now unless the Lord decides differently.

Coats Shoeing is a family affair and they all pitch in and help when Gary needs it. Son-in-law
Andy Lumpkin lives close by and helps Gary daily with the trimming and shoeing. Another
son-in-law, Caleb Felts is able to help when he can and Kipp Harrell pitches in from time to
time as well.

Along with his wife of 42 years, Gary and Sharon have three daughters. Kasey is married
to Andy and they have one son, Levi, who is known to strap on his shoeing chaps and help
Pappy out with the rasping. Daughter Kelly teaches school and works part time as a vet tech
and Kirby is married to Caleb and they have a boy and girl, Eli and Piper; who also help
Pappy in the barn.
